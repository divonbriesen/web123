Introduction Form (M12 Collecting Data / M13 HTML/JSON/XML Out)

Build a form that lets a visitor create their own introduction page — Part 1 (M12) collects the data and rebuilds the page on submit; Part 2 (M13) adds raw HTML, JSON, and XML output. AI expected on both.

The Acid Test (how we evaluate M12)

  • We follow your link and click Submit untouched — the result must be indistinguishable from your real introduction page (spacing, colors, fonts, footer links) except the h2 says "Introduction Form" and the title matches.
  • We edit prefilled values — the new values must appear on submit, including a different uploaded image (the most-missed feature: your image is the default, but an upload overrides it).
  • We clear the form — optional fields may stay blank; the rest must be required.
  • We add and remove courses — JS must add course field groups (department, number, name, reason) with a delete button beside each.

Setup & Form (M12)

  • introduction_form.html in your itis3135 folder, linked from your common primary menu (one place — your header is a component by now). Title and h2 follow the usual pattern.
  • h3: "Please submit the form below."
  • One form asking for everything on the introduction: first/middle(opt)/nickname(opt)/last name, acknowledgment statement and date, mascot adjective and animal, divider, picture (defaulting to yours, uploadable), picture caption, personal statement, the 7 main bullets, courses with department/number/name/reason each, quote and author, funny thing (opt), something to share (opt), and your 5 footer links.
  • Prefill your values with the value attribute; add placeholders that show when cleared.
  • Buttons: Submit (type="submit"), Reset (type="reset"), and Clear (type="button", empties every field).
  • scripts/introduction.js: prevent submit without required fields, prevent the default page refresh, reset progress, add/remove course rows, and on submit swap the form for the built introduction — with a reset link at the bottom so a visitor can go again.

Generate Output (M13)

  • Add buttons — Generate HTML, Generate JSON, Generate XML, in that order.
  • Each appends a text area at the bottom holding the raw code for whatever is currently in the form — nicely formatted JSON, the HTML itself as code, and an XML representation. Change the form, click again, and the output reflects it.
  • Everything from M12 must keep working alongside.

Submitting (each part)

  • The URL of your form page.
  • A brief summary of what you did, what you asked AI to do, and impressions.
  • Peer reviews: these instructions as a GO/NO-GO checklist on at least two classmates who don't already have two reviews.

What gets checked: the page is in the primary nav; the form has 15+ inputs with prefilled values and placeholders, a file-upload input, Submit/Reset/Clear buttons, and scripts/introduction.js; from M13 on, the Generate HTML/JSON/XML buttons. The submit-result fidelity is judged by eye against your real introduction.
