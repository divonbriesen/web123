freeCodeCamp & Codecademy Setup/Start (M0e)

Create the two practice-platform accounts you'll use through the semester, make your progress publicly visible, and do the first bit of work on each.

Accounts

  • Create accounts on freeCodeCamp and Codecademy — ideally with the Google account tied to your school account.
  • Your username must include your name, initials, or mascot — something that identifies it as you, since we cross-reference with Canvas. Add whatever else you like to the profiles.

Make It Visible

  • Set both profiles public — points, timeline, certifications, activity heatmap; anything that demonstrates the work.
  • Test with a private/incognito window: open the URL you're about to submit. If it works there, we can see it. If it doesn't, we can't — and can't grade it.

Do the First Work

  • freeCodeCamp: earn at least 10 points.
  • Codecademy: complete the Welcome to Codecademy lesson.

Submitting

  • Your freeCodeCamp profile URL showing timeline, certifications, 10+ points, and activity.
  • Your Codecademy profile URL showing the Welcome lesson completed.
  • Make the URLs clickable — edit the text if needed. Submit profile URLs, not codecademy.com/learn or another generic page.
  • Add any notes you'd care to share.

What gets checked: the freeCodeCamp profile is public with 10+ points; the Codecademy profile is public with the Welcome lesson done. No partial score — 1/20 means fix things asap, not "5% done."
