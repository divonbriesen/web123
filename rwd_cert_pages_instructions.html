RWD Certification Pages (M6B Setup / M9 Build Out)

A two-part assignment pairing your course-site building with the freeCodeCamp RWD certification projects. Part A (M6B) frames in the pages; Part B (M9) builds them out around a theme of yours. Careful use of AI is encouraged — don't trust it, but leverage it.

The Five Pages

  • survey.html — Build a Survey Form (FCC's HTML section). Heads-up: later you'll build a form collecting introduction info — seed some of those inputs here where they fit.
  • cards.html — Build a Page of Playing Cards (images themed to you, not actual playing cards)
  • inventory.html — Build a Book Inventory App (things from your theme: products, prizes, ideas)
  • documentation.html — Build a Technical Documentation Page
  • product.html — Build a Product Landing Page

Part A (M6B) — Setup

  • Create all five as empty framed pages: common header/footer components, default.css linked, validation in place, and an h2 in main saying what the content will be — nothing else.
  • Link them in your primary navbar, in order, after Home and Contract.
  • Make a second navbar for anything not themed like your course site — move your CRAPpy page link there, plus client sites and any other off-theme links, now and in the future.

Part B (M9) — Build Out

  • Every page reflects one consistent theme tied to you, your mascot, or an idea the mascot's name suggests. Think village pub: the Salty Dog has nothing to do with salt or dogs, and it works. Dapper Vicuna might sell deep-fried Oreos — then every page relates to deep-fried Oreos. Pick something entertaining or meaningful so the pages amuse you. (You may change your mascot if the initials still match.)
  • Inspiration: the 826 Valencia pirate-store TED talk, or Bubba's shrimp list from Forrest Gump.
  • Every page has the Accumulus validator, all tests passing, including WCAG (click the cloud) — contrast issues are the classic miss.

Submitting (both parts)

  • Your course URL — the pages evident in the menu.
  • Notes welcome; questions go on the class discussion board instead, preferably before submitting.
  • Peer reviews: these instructions as a GO/NO-GO checklist on at least two classmates who don't already have two reviews.

What gets checked: M6B — all five pages exist and the primary nav links them. M9 — each page is built out: survey has a form with 3+ inputs, cards has 6+ themed images, inventory has a table of rows, documentation has 3+ sections, product has sections plus an image — with an h2 naming every page.
