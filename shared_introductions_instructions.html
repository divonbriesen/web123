Introductions Instructions

Shared Google Doc Workflow

This page condenses the course introduction document into the required steps, the required content, and the formatting rules that matter for the shared document and its table of contents.

Part 1: Create And Link Your Profile

  1. Open the Introductions Google Doc from the course front page.
  2. Go to the main body and place your entry alphabetically by last name.
  3. Type your name as Last Name, First Name Middle Initial.
  4. Format that line as Heading 2.
  5. Refresh the table of contents and test that the link jumps to your entry.
  6. Copy the URL of that Heading 2 entry to submit later in the assignment.

Part 2: Add Your Personal Information

  • Write a short public-acknowledgment sentence, then add your initials and date in italics.
  • On a new line, add your display name in the form First M. "Nickname" Last if applicable.
  • Add an adjective-animal mascot matching your initials, separated with a symbol like ~, |, or *.
  • Make that entire display-name line large and centered, but do not make it a heading.
  • Insert a centered photo of yourself and add a centered italic caption beneath it.
  • Write a left-justified personal statement of 3 or more complete sentences.

Part 3: Add Background And Course Information

Use a bulleted list and make the text before each colon bold.

  • Personal Background: Brief summary.
  • Professional Background: Brief summary.
  • Academic Background: Major, year, or academic path.
  • Primary Computer: Manufacturer, operating system, device type, and main work location.
  • Courses I'm Taking, & Why:
    • CRS101 - Course Name: Reason for taking it.
    • CRS201 - Course Name: Reason for taking it.
    • ITIS3135 - Front-End Web Development: Reason for taking it.
  • Funny/Interesting Item to Remember Me by: Optional detail.
  • I'd Also Like to Share: Optional detail.

Leave one blank line before the quote only.

Part 4: Complete The Online Assignment

  1. Update your profile picture in the online course.
  2. Find the Introductions discussion or assignment.
  3. Create the submission using the URL of your Heading 2 entry.
  4. Revise and reply to feedback until you receive full credit if needed.

Common Errors: Avoid These Mistakes

  • Do not alphabetize by first name.
  • Do not make the big centered display name a heading.
  • Do not center with spaces; use the alignment tools.
  • Do not create a paragraph below a bullet when a line break is needed.
  • Do not forget nested bullets for the course list.
  • Do not underline anything except actual links.
  • Do not add extra blank lines beyond the one before the quote.
